A Light in the Dark

Part One

It was a dark night. A young man named Jason was walking along a path. He wanted to get to a village, but it was hard for him to find his way in the dark.

Suddenly, he saw a light in the dark. A man with a torch was coming toward him. Jason decided to wait for the man with the torch. "Maybe he'll agree to take me to the village," he said to himself.

Jason waited for the man, but the man was walking very slowly. Jason saw that he had a cane.

A Light in the Dark

Part Two

"Good evening," the man said. "Come with me. I'll take you to the village."

"It took you a long time to get here," Jason said.

"Sorry, but as you see, I'm blind. This is why I walk slowly."

"Blind? Are you blind? Then what do you need this torch for?" Jason asked and laughed. "A blind man doesn't need a torch."

"My torch is not for me. It's for people who can see. My torch is for people like you who cannot find their way in the dark. I know how you feel when you cannot see. It is important for me to help others."

That day, Jason learned an important lesson. He learned to put himself in other people's shoes and try to understand their needs and feelings.

💭 קראו את שני הדיאלוגים. מי הוא/היא אדם מסוג "YOU" — Guy או Sue? הסבירו את תשובתכם בכתב.
💭 Think About It – "I" People vs "YOU" People
There are two kinds of people – "I" people and "YOU" people.
"I" people say: "I want..." / "I need..." / "I feel..."
"YOU" people say: "What do you want?" / "What do you need?" / "How do you feel?"

Who is a "YOU" person — Guy or Sue? Explain your answer.

Guy: Mom, I need a new smartphone.
Mother: I know, but we can't afford it now. I hope you understand.
Guy: I don't want to understand. I want a new smartphone. Why don't you borrow some money from Grandma?
Betty: My family and I are going to Eilat for the weekend. Come with us!
Sue: I can't. You know that my little brother is a child with special needs. He needs me. And my mom needs my help. She can't take care of him all by herself.
DWho is the "YOU" person — Guy or Sue? Explain.
Attempts left: 2

The Legend of the Hummingbird

by Pierre Rabhi

The animals were in the forest. The forest was their home.

Suddenly, a fire broke out in the forest. The animals were afraid, but there was nothing they could do to stop the fire.

A little hummingbird had an idea. "Water! I'll bring water to stop the fire," the bird thought.

Quickly, the little bird flew to the spring, picked up a drop of water in its beak, flew back and threw the drop of water on the fire.

Again and again, the bird flew to the spring, brought drops of water and threw them on the fire.

"What are you doing?" the animals asked. "You can't stop the fire with these drops of water!"

"I know", the bird said, "but I'm doing my part."
